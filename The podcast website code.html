ABOUT THE SHOW

Professional Perspectives started as a simple idea between two high school friends — Aarav Akella and Praneeth Vemuri — who wanted to learn directly from people making an impact in the real world.

What began as curiosity has grown into a platform that bridges the gap between students and the professional world. Through thoughtful questions and genuine dialogue, every episode uncovers the personal journeys behind each guest's success: the challenges they faced, the decisions that defined them, and the lessons they wish they'd known earlier.

Whether it's a startup founder sharing how they built something from scratch, a coach discussing leadership and resilience, or an executive revealing what drives their success, Professional Perspectives offers a rare, human look into what it takes to thrive in any field.

Aimed at high school and college students — and anyone eager to learn from experience rather than textbooks — every episode leaves listeners not just inspired, but equipped with practical takeaways they can apply to their own ambitions.

YOUR HOSTS

The curious minds behind the mic

Professional Perspectives is built on curiosity, connection, and the belief that stories have the power to inspire change. Meet the two high school students who started it all.

AA
AARAV AKELLA
Co-Host & Co-Founder

A high school student with a passion for business, technology, and understanding what drives people to build extraordinary things. Aarav brings sharp curiosity and a knack for getting to the heart of every guest's story. He believes that the best way to learn about a career isn't from a textbook — it's from the people who actually lived it.

PV
PRANEETH VEMURI
Co-Host & Co-Founder

Equally driven by curiosity about the professional world, Praneeth brings a thoughtful, empathetic approach to every conversation. He has a gift for making guests feel comfortable opening up about the harder parts of their journeys — the failures, the pivots, and the moments of doubt that ultimately led to success.

"We started this podcast because we were two students who wanted better answers than 'study hard and figure it out.' Every guest we talk to gives us — and our listeners — a clearer picture of what's possible."

— Aarav & Praneeth

PRIVACY POLICY

Effective date: June 11, 2026 (version 2026-06-11)

Who we are

Professional Perspectives Podcast is the data controller for personal information collected through this website. For anything in this policy, contact us at professionalperspectivespr@gmail.com.

What we collect, why, and on what basis

When you use the contact form, we collect the following categories of personal information: identifiers (your first and last name, email address) and the free-text content of your message, solely so the hosts can read and respond to it. The legal basis is your consent, given via the checkbox on the form. To protect the form from abuse, we also process your IP address; a keyed cryptographic hash of it is stored briefly for rate limiting and deleted within hours. The legal basis for abuse prevention is our legitimate interest in keeping the form operational. We do not use cookies, analytics, or advertising trackers on this site.

Who we share it with

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share it with third parties for their direct marketing purposes (California Civil Code §1798.83, "Shine the Light"). Your submission is processed by the providers that run this site: Netlify (hosting, form processing, and short-lived function logs), Neon (database storage), Cloudflare (Turnstile bot protection on the form), and Google (Gmail — each submission is also delivered as an email notification to our inbox). These providers process data only on our behalf. All of them are based in the United States; if you submit from outside the US, your information will be transferred to and processed in the US.

Our newsletter (Substack)

Our newsletter is run separately on Substack. You are added to it only if you subscribe there yourself — the contact form on this site never adds you to the newsletter or to any other mailing list. Data you provide when subscribing on Substack is governed by Substack's privacy policy, and every newsletter email includes an unsubscribe link.

How long we keep it

Contact submissions are deleted from our database automatically 90 days after you send them, and rate-limiting hashes are deleted within hours. Two caveats, in the interest of honesty: a copy of each submission is delivered by email to our Gmail inbox, where it is kept until we have responded and is deleted on request; and deleted database records may persist in our database provider's encrypted backups for a limited period before aging out. A deletion request covers both the database and our inbox copy.

Your rights and how we verify requests

You may request a copy, correction, or earlier deletion of anything you submitted by emailing professionalperspectivespr@gmail.com. We verify requests by requiring that they come from the same email address used in the original submission; we will not act on requests about another person's data. We aim to respond within 30 days. If you are in the EU/EEA or UK, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.

Do Not Track

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. This site does not respond to DNT signals — there is nothing to opt out of, as we do not track visitors across this or other websites.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to how we collect or use personal information, we will update this page, the version, and the effective date above before the changes take effect.
